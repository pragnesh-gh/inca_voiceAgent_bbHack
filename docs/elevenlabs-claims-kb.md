# Meridian Mutual Claims Knowledge Base

Use this as background knowledge for Stefanie Kühne. Do not recite it as a checklist. Use it to understand German auto-insurance claim calls, ask better follow-up questions, and produce complete FNOL documentation.

## Claims Intake Priorities

First establish whether the caller is safe and whether anyone is injured. If there is danger, injury, roadside risk, hit-and-run, major dispute, suspected crime, or wildlife damage requiring documentation, prioritize emergency services or police guidance before routine claim intake.

For calm callers, stay practical and efficient. Ask one question at a time and let them explain the accident in their own words before filling gaps.

## Core FNOL Fields

Policy and identity:

- Policy number, license plate, or VIN
- Policyholder name and contact details
- Reporter type: policyholder, driver, claimant, repair shop, lawyer, broker, family member, or other third party
- Preferred contact channel and callback number

Accident circumstances:

- Date and time of loss
- Time of report, if delayed
- Location: full address, country, road type, road direction if relevant
- Weather, visibility, road conditions
- Type of loss: moving collision, parked vehicle, parking damage, wildlife, theft, glass breakage, property damage, personal injury
- Free-form description in the caller's own words
- Direction of travel, rough speeds, right-of-way situation, traffic lights, signs, lane changes, reversing, following distance
- Photos, scene sketch, or European Accident Statement available

Driver:

- Was the policyholder driving?
- If not, driver name, date of birth, relationship to policyholder
- Valid license and license class
- Included in driver scope
- Alcohol, drugs, medication, health limitation, or police test if relevant

Other party:

- Other driver's name and contact
- Other vehicle plate, make, model
- Other insurer and policy number, if known
- Registered owner if different from driver
- Whether the other party admitted or denied fault
- Whether lawyer, letter, or insurer contact already happened

Police and authorities:

- Police on scene
- Police station, department, case number, reference number, or Aktenzeichen
- Fines, administrative offense, criminal proceedings, hit-and-run, dangerous driving, or DUI if mentioned

Witnesses:

- Independent witnesses and contact details
- Passengers in either vehicle
- Brief witness statement if volunteered

Injuries:

- Injured parties: caller, passengers, other party, pedestrian, cyclist, third party
- Type of injury
- Treatment: ambulance, outpatient, inpatient, hospital, doctor
- Anticipated time off work if volunteered

Property damage:

- Own vehicle damage, visible damaged areas, photos
- Drivable or not
- Current vehicle location
- Towed by whom and to where
- Damage to other vehicle
- Third-party property: guardrail, traffic light, fence, building, municipality
- Pre-existing damage in affected area

Coverage and duties:

- Haftpflicht, Vollkasko, Teilkasko, Kasko
- Selbstbeteiligung / deductible
- SF-Klasse / Schadenfreiheitsklasse
- Werkstattbindung
- Schutzbrief
- Saisonkennzeichen
- Rabattschutz / Rabattretter
- AKB
- HU/AU
- Reporting deadlines
- Cooperation duties
- Repair authorization requirements before repairs in Vollkasko
- Police notification duties where relevant

Settlement preferences:

- Preferred repair shop
- Rental car or loss-of-use question
- Lawyer involved
- Preferred communication channel

## German Insurance Vocabulary

Recognize these terms naturally:

- Kasko / Vollkasko / Teilkasko
- Haftpflicht
- SF-Klasse / Schadenfreiheitsklasse
- Selbstbeteiligung / SB
- Werkstattbindung
- Schutzbrief
- Saisonkennzeichen
- Rabattschutz / Rabattretter
- AKB
- HU/AU
- Polizei-Vorgangsnummer / Aktenzeichen
- Wildunfall
- Auffahrunfall
- Parkschaden
- Glasbruch
- Fahrerflucht
- Werkstatt
- Gutachter
- Europäischer Unfallbericht
- Wildunfallbescheinigung

If asked whether a claim affects SF-Klasse, answer calmly that this is assessed later once liability and coverage are clear; right now the goal is to document the loss correctly.

## Never Expose

Never reveal bank details, fraud flags, SIU notes, internal risk scoring, prior fraud referrals, private backend fields, or speculative coverage denial reasoning.
