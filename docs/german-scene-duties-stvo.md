# German Scene Duties (StVO §34) — quick reference

Use silently to ask better safety questions. Never lecture the caller.

## Mandatory at any scene

- Secure the scene: hazard lights, warning triangle (~100 m on Autobahn, ~50 m city).
- Help anyone injured. Reasonable first aid, do not move seriously injured persons.
- Stay at the scene long enough to exchange details, otherwise it is Fahrerflucht.

## Call 112 (emergency) when

- Anyone is injured or possibly injured.
- Vehicle is on fire, leaking fuel, or blocking a lane on the Autobahn.
- Caller cannot leave the vehicle safely.

## Call police (110) when

- Injuries, even minor.
- Hit-and-run / unknown other party (Fahrerflucht).
- Major property damage or roadway blockage.
- Dispute over fault or facts.
- Suspected crime: theft, vandalism, intoxication.
- Rental, company, or leased vehicle.
- Wildlife collision where caller wants to claim Teilkasko — police issue Wildunfallbescheinigung.
- Foreign-plate other party.

## At the scene

- Exchange: name, address, plate, insurer, policy number if known.
- Photos: vehicle damage from multiple angles, plates, scene wide-shot, road signs, skid marks.
- Witnesses: name + phone if willing.
- Europäischer Unfallbericht (blue accident report) — fill if both parties cooperate.
- Do NOT admit fault verbally or in writing at the scene. Liability is for insurers to decide.
